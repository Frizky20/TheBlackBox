# LinkedIn Authority Frameworks: B2B Outreach & Personalization

## 1. The "Anti-Pitch" Framework (Guillaume Moubeche / Lemlist)
**Core Logic:** Stop trying to sell in the first message. Sell the "Conversation," not the "Product."

* **The Structure:**
    1. **The Specific Hook:** Mention a recent milestone or a specific detail found in their 10-K or LinkedIn post.
    2. **The "Tension" Point:** Highlight a common industry problem (e.g., "Most SaaS companies struggle with LTV/CAC in Indonesia").
    3. **The Soft CTA:** "I wrote a brief breakdown on how [Company X] solved this. Would you be opposed to me sending it over?"
* **Key takeaway:** High-reply rates come from lowering the "threat level" of the message.

## 2. Programmatic Personalization (Eric Nowoslawski / Growth Outreach)
**Core Logic:** Using AI and data scraping to make 1,000 emails feel like 1-on-1 conversations.

* **The Framework:**
    1. **Scrape:** Find a unique data point (e.g., "I noticed you’re currently hiring for [Role]").
    2. **Synthesize:** Link that role to a specific pain point.
    3. **The "Value Bomb":** "Usually, when companies hire for [Role], they are trying to scale [Process]. I built a system that automates the first 40% of that."
* **Key takeaway:** Personalization isn't just a name; it's showing you understand their current internal "Delta" (the gap between where they are and where they want to be).

## 3. The "Educational Hybrid" (Regional Playbook)
**Core Logic:** Adapted for the Indonesian market (Suwandi Soh / Mekari influence).

* **The Framework:**
    1. **The Relationship Hook:** Acknowledge the regional ecosystem (e.g., "Seeing the growth of the SaaS scene in BSD/South Tangerang...").
    2. **The Compliance/Trust Signal:** Mention localized success or security (e.g., "How we handled localized payroll integration for [X]").
    3. **The Low-Friction CTA:** "Would love to exchange notes on the regional GTM landscape."
