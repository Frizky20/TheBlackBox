# Will Allred: Copywriting Frameworks (LinkedIn)

## Framework 1: “Why you → Why now → One ask”
- **Post Template**
  - Why you: one specific reason this is relevant to *them*
  - Why now: one trigger (timing, change, constraint)
  - One ask: one question that’s easy to answer
- **The Psychology**
  - Relevance + timing reduces “random outreach” suspicion; one ask reduces decision fatigue.
- **Result**
  - More “sure / curious / send it” replies vs silent ignores.

## Framework 2: “Clarity Stack (subject + first line + CTA)”
- **Post Template**
  - Subject: neutral, non-salesy (often 1–3 words)
  - First line: specific observation, not a compliment
  - CTA: “open loop” question (“Worth exploring?” / “Should I send details?”)
- **The Psychology**
  - People decide in seconds. These three lines carry most of the conversion weight.
- **Result**
  - Higher read-through and fewer spam complaints because it feels conversational.

## Framework 3: “Cut the fluff (de-claim, de-jargon, de-scope)”
- **Post Template**
  - Remove claims (“best”, “leading”) and replace with concrete outcomes
  - Remove jargon and replace with plain verbs
  - Reduce scope to one use case
- **The Psychology**
  - Claims trigger skepticism; specifics trigger belief.
- **Result**
  - Higher trust density per word → better reply rate at the same send volume.

## 100Hires Application
100Hires can use these to increase recruiter/outbound response rates without increasing volume:
- Use “why you/why now” triggers (hiring surges, new HR leader, expansion) to make outreach timely.
- Optimize the first 3 lines to protect deliverability indirectly (better engagement → better inboxing).
- Narrow each email to one hiring workflow outcome (screening speed, shortlist quality, compliance handling) to reduce skepticism.
