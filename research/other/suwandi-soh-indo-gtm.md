# Suwandi Soh: Indonesia SME Trust + Compliance Playbook (BPJS/HR)

## Tactical Notes (Practitioner-Level)

### Trust-building in Indonesian SMEs (what actually gets a “yes”)
- **Local proof > global proof**: Indonesian SME buyers overweight local references (same city/industry/regulatory reality) over international logos.
- **Risk framing beats upside framing**: Position around avoiding payroll errors, compliance penalties, and “audit pain” more than generic productivity.
- **Relationship motion matters**: Warm intros, community ties, and ongoing education content outperform one-shot pitches in under-digitized segments.
- **Channel reality**: Expect WhatsApp as a serious business channel for follow-up and coordination; use email for formal artifacts.

### Compliance wedge: HR/payroll specifics that drive urgency
- **BPJS as a forcing function**: BPJS (Kesehatan/Ketenagakerjaan) obligations create recurring operational risk; the buyer wants confidence the workflow is correct every month.
- **Payroll correctness**: SMEs care about “no surprises” (net pay accuracy, cutoff times, change logs) more than feature lists.
- **Auditability**: The buyer wants a clear trail (who changed what, when) and exportability for accountants/tax consultants.

### Practical GTM implications for your pipeline
- Build a “compliance-first” inbound/outbound narrative:
  - “Here’s the 5 mistakes that cause BPJS/payslip disputes” → then show the fix.
- Add **education assets** as pipeline accelerators:
  - BPJS checklist, payroll SOP template, month-end closing steps, sample audit log.
- Segment by maturity:
  - Spreadsheet HR → “stability + compliance”
  - Existing HRIS → “migration safety + integration”
