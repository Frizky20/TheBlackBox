# Kyle Coleman: Pipeline Hygiene + SDR/AE Alignment Metrics

## Metrics that keep pipeline “clean” (and why they matter)

### 1) Stage Conversion Integrity
- Track: **Lead → Meeting held → Qualified → Pipeline created → Closed-won**
- Watch for: meetings that don’t become qualified pipeline (symptom of poor targeting or weak handoffs).

### 2) Speed-to-Lead and Speed-to-Follow-up
- Track: median minutes to first response and days between touches.
- Reason: latency kills win rates; also signals operational discipline.

### 3) Disqualification Taxonomy
- Track: top disqual reasons (no budget, wrong segment, timing, competitor, compliance).
- Reason: if “wrong persona / wrong segment” is high, marketing + SDR targeting is misaligned.

### 4) SDR → AE Handoff Quality
- Track: % of meetings accepted by AE, and “recycled” opps (AE rejects / returns to SDR).
- Reason: acceptance rate is the fastest proxy for alignment and meeting quality.

### 5) Pipeline Coverage vs Capacity
- Track: pipeline created per AE vs quota, plus stage-weighted coverage.
- Reason: prevents “activity theater” when pipeline quality is low.

## Alignment Operating Rhythm (weekly)
- 30 min SDR+AE review:
  - 5 best meetings (what to repeat)
  - 5 worst meetings (what to stop)
  - update ICP rules and disqual taxonomy
- Marketing joins to map:
  - what messages produce accepted meetings
  - what channels produce qualified pipeline

## 100Hires Application
100Hires can use these metrics to scale revenue without bloating the funnel:
- **Quality control**: AE acceptance + recycle rate gives a fast signal if outbound targeting is drifting.
- **Operational discipline**: speed-to-lead standards reduce lost deals and improve prospect experience.
- **Alignment**: disqual taxonomy turns “sales complaints” into measurable input for better ICP segmentation and messaging.
