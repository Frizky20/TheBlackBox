# Justin Welsh: Distribution Frameworks (LinkedIn)

## Framework 1: “Point of View → Proof → CTA”
- **Post Template**
  - Line 1: a sharp POV (“Most people think X. That’s why they fail.”)
  - 3–6 lines: proof via a metric, screenshot, or simple before/after
  - Close: one-step CTA (“Want the checklist? Comment ‘X’ and I’ll send it.”)
- **The Psychology**
  - Authority is earned by **committing to a belief** and backing it with proof.
  - Low-friction CTA turns passive readers into identifiable leads.
- **Result**
  - Higher comment velocity → more distribution; plus a clean “hand-raise” list for follow-up.

## Framework 2: “System Post (inputs → process → outputs)”
- **Post Template**
  - “Here’s the system I use to achieve X.”
  - Bullet the *inputs* (tools, time, constraints), then the *process*, then the *outputs*
  - End with “steal this” angle (template/checklist)
- **The Psychology**
  - Systems reduce uncertainty; readers don’t want motivation, they want **repeatability**.
- **Result**
  - Saves-to-impressions ratio rises; posts become evergreen acquisition assets.

## Framework 3: “Objection Flip (why the common advice fails)”
- **Post Template**
  - State common advice
  - Explain why it fails in real life
  - Replace with a simpler rule + example
- **The Psychology**
  - “Permission to disagree” creates attention; the example creates trust.
- **Result**
  - Stronger follow rate from people who align with the POV; tighter audience → better downstream conversion.

## 100Hires Application
100Hires can use these distribution frameworks to warm outbound and reduce CAC:
- Publish a consistent POV about “hiring operations” (speed, quality, compliance) with proof (time-to-fill deltas, response rates, case outcomes).
- Use comment CTAs to build segmented lead lists (e.g., “Comment ‘BPJS’ for the Indonesia checklist”).
- Turn internal playbooks into “system posts” that pre-sell process credibility before outreach.
