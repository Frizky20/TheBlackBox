## The Checklist (Go / No-Go)

**Report:** This checklist is the operational control for outbound.**states only—no selling language.** If any required gate is not green, **hold or abort** that row, cohort, or campaign until the data is fixed.

**Rule:** Do not advance to send until **all three gates below read green.**

| Gate | Green (Go) | Red (No-Go / Hold) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Hiring intent** | On-record evidence of hiring posture or talent pressure we can quote in one clause (open reqs, recruiter expansion, leadership statements). List reason and message must match—same roster logic, same sentence. (source: Jordan Crawford, https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/inverting-your-go-to-market-strategy-thinking-with-jordan-crawford-ep-55/transcript from 17.05.2025) | Conflicting reqs, stale listings, obvious RPO noise, or firmographics-only guesses. **Flag as a bad lead** until verified. |
| **Tech stack match** | Primary-source proof from site or stack/integration pages that our product legitimately fits—**evidence stored in the row.** (source: Eric Nowoslawski, research/other/eric-nowoslawski-programmatic.md from 2025) | Logo guessing, contradictory tools, nothing we could defend in a live call. **Flag as a bad lead.** |
| **Trigger event** | One time-bound catalyst (leadership change, expansion signal, budget/compliance window, localization push) turned into a short, factual hook—paired with a permission ask or micro-audit, not a blind pitch. (source: Eric Nowoslawski, research/other/eric-nowoslawski-programmatic.md from 2025) · (source: Guillaume Moubeche, research/linkedin-posts/guillaume-moubeche-psychology.md from 2025) | “Large company ⇒ must need us” with no dated fact. **Hold.** |

**Addendum — Indonesia / SME:** Require **local trust corroboration** (peer reference path, compliance context) before leaning on WhatsApp; keep formal record in email. (source: Suwandi Soh, research/other/suwandi-soh-indo-gtm.md from 2025)

**Director note:** This checklist overrides enthusiasm from any other section. If infra and copy are ready but gates are red, **we do not launch.**

## Master SOP — Outreach roadmap by phase

### Phase 1 — Strategy

Pick the fight before you buy the tools.

- **Start with one narrow segment.** If you won't choose who *not* to sell to, you get generic outbound, weak replies, and more reputation risk for no gain. (source: Jordan Crawford, https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/inverting-your-go-to-market-strategy-thinking-with-jordan-crawford-ep-55/transcript from 17.05.2025)
- **Work from a Permissionless Value Prop:** lead with insight or proof the buyer didn't ask for—not a demo request—as the hook for relevance. (source: Jordan Crawford, https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/inverting-your-go-to-market-strategy-thinking-with-jordan-crawford-ep-55/transcript from 17.05.2025)
- **Let the list tell you what to say.** If everyone on the sheet is there for the same factual reason (same signal), the message stays honest and repeatable. (source: Jordan Crawford, https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/inverting-your-go-to-market-strategy-thinking-with-jordan-crawford-ep-55/transcript from 17.05.2025)
- **Write down upfront criteria for rejection** (budget, wrong person, competitor lock-in, timing, compliance). Review them weekly with sales so bad leads don't inflate “activity.” (source: Kyle Coleman, research/other/kyle-coleman-alignment.md from 2025)
- **Track funnel quality, not vibes:** Lead → Meeting → Qualified → Pipeline → Won; flag where meetings vaporize—usually targeting or messaging drift. (source: Kyle Coleman, research/other/kyle-coleman-alignment.md from 2025)
- **Indonesia / similar SME wedges:** prioritize local proof, payroll and compliance stakes (including BPJS), relationship rhythm, WhatsApp for coordination, email for formal paper. (source: Suwandi Soh, research/other/suwandi-soh-indo-gtm.md from 2025)

### Phase 2 — Enrichment (Clay-style)

Build lists as if QA is the product—you're assembling evidence, not names.

- **One sentence hypothesis before Clay:** “If they show X, they probably struggle with Y”—then only enrich fields that prove or disprove it. (source: Eric Nowoslawski, research/other/eric-nowoslawski-programmatic.md from 2025)
- **Clean company, domain, LinkedIn URLs, and titles before any mail merge.** Sloppy joins read as bots. (source: Eric Nowoslawski, research/other/eric-nowoslawski-programmatic.md from 2025)
- **Scrape for triggers:** careers pages, integrations/stack pages, announcements—turn them into booleans plus a one-line snippet of proof. (source: Eric Nowoslawski, research/other/eric-nowoslawski-programmatic.md from 2025)
- **Verify email; separate valid, risky, and catch-all.** Run catch-all cohorts separately so bounces don't torch the primary pool—**separate risk** at the list level, not only in copy. (source: Eric Nowoslawski, research/other/eric-nowoslawski-programmatic.md from 2025)
- **QA before export:** drop rows missing trigger proof, blown token fields, noisy caps, junk titles—then export columns that match the sender tool exactly. (source: Eric Nowoslawski, research/other/eric-nowoslawski-programmatic.md from 2025)

### Phase 3 — Infrastructure

Assume domains and mailboxes burn; design for graceful failure.

- **Treat reputation as stacking:** sender IP/server → aged domain → mailbox. Diversity beats hero domains. (source: Lead Gen Jay, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJbLvafsCx4 from 14.06.2024)
- **SPF, DKIM, DMARC:** fix before measuring anything else; test headers on seed sends. (source: Nick Abraham, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h2j0gFz9RH4 from 23.02.2026)
- **Prefer mailbox setups buyers already trust (Google / Microsoft workspaces); avoid fragile SMTP setups that die on filter swings.** (source: Nick Abraham, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h2j0gFz9RH4 from 23.02.2026)
- **Warm-up:** minimum about two weeks; plan closer to six weeks when you need the asset to live longer; add redundancy if you're in a hurry. (source: Nick Abraham, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h2j0gFz9RH4 from 23.02.2026)
- **Let domains age (~30 days) before leaning on heavy volume.** (source: Lead Gen Jay, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJbLvafsCx4 from 14.06.2024)
- **Scale by adding mailboxes/domains at conservative daily caps—not by brute-forcing one inbox.** Use **varied sending times** and human-looking ramps, not rigid machine-perfect patterns. (source: Lead Gen Jay, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJbLvafsCx4 from 14.06.2024)
- **Plain text posture; rotate two infra pools monthly; keep spare non-brand domains ready to swap** when replies or placement slip. Treat **open rates under ~40%** as “stop and fix infra/list,” not “send louder.” (source: Nick Abraham, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h2j0gFz9RH4 from 23.02.2026)
- **Separate strict security-gateway companies** (e.g., Proofpoint-style stacks surfaced via DNS) so they don't spike bounces across healthy sends. Split **verified vs catch-all** sends. Validate before launch. (source: Nick Abraham, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h2j0gFz9RH4 from 23.02.2026)
- **If you insist on tracked links/unsubs, isolate them on custom tracking domains** so one policy link doesn't stain the sending domain—but see *Open Tracking* below for our standard. (source: Lead Gen Jay, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJbLvafsCx4 from 14.06.2024)

### Phase 4 — Psychology

Short sequences, calm copy, proof over hype.

- **Prefer a compact ~10 business-day rhythm:** email opener, light social touch, bump, single “permissionless value” audit moment, restrained follow-up, polite breakup—no endless chains. (source: Chris Ritson, research/other/chris-ritson-systems.md from 2026)
- **First emails stay tight:** neutral subject; first line is a specific observation, not flattery; one easy question—not a brochure. (source: Will Allred, research/linkedin-posts/will-allred-copywriting.md from 2025)
- **Structure touches as:** why this company, why now, one ask—and strip fluff, jargon, and “we're the leader” filler. (source: Will Allred, research/linkedin-posts/will-allred-copywriting.md from 2025)
- **When buyers are guarded:** permission-based asks (“worth a five-line breakdown?”) and tiny audits beat forced calendar grabs. Stories that show how you stopped doing dumb things beat chest-thumping. (source: Guillaume Moubeche, research/linkedin-posts/guillaume-moubeche-psychology.md from 2025)
- **Content distribution (LinkedIn)** can warm the market: point of view + proof + a clear—but not gimmicky—CTA; “systems” posts (inputs → process → outputs) build trust before outbound lands. Treat distribution as seasoning, not a substitute for infra discipline. (source: Justin Welsh, research/linkedin-posts/justin-welsh-distribution.md from 2025)

If Legal ever mandates links, isolate them using Jay's custom-tracking approach—but Nick's stance wins for recruiter-grade trust and inbox placement. |

## What i rejected — and why

1. **Generic flattery openings (“Love what you're doing…”).** Hiring leaders see it fifty times a day; it wastes the subject line gamble and trains recipients to lump us with noisy vendors—which lands us in spam reflexes faster than any infra slip. We're paid to show we did homework, not that we noticed their LinkedIn photo. (source: Will Allred, research/linkedin-posts/will-allred-copywriting.md from 2025)

2. **Cheap “scale” infra (risky VPS / sketchy warmed mailboxes)** and shipping big volume through them. One bad cohort can crater shared IP reputation and ripple across dozens of domains. Short-term dopamine isn’t worth a year recovering domain trust. (source: Lead Gen Jay, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJbLvafsCx4 from 14.06.2024)

3. **Clay theater without hypotheses—enrich everything, mail everyone.** Vanity columns create confident-sounding hallucinations at scale and spike complaints. If the enrichment row can't answer “why them, why now” with a stored proof snippet, it's a no-send. (source: Eric Nowoslawski, research/other/eric-nowoslawski-programmatic.md from 2025)

## Weaknesses & risks of this playbook.

- **Labor-heavy QA:** gated enrichment catches garbage but costs operator hours—small teams bottleneck before prospects ever see mail. (source: Eric Nowoslawski, research/other/eric-nowoslawski-programmatic.md from 2025) · (source: Chris Ritson, research/other/chris-ritson-systems.md from 2026)
- **Cash before proof:** rotated domains, warm timelines, spare pools—we pay rent on safety before dashboards look exciting. (source: Nick Abraham, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h2j0gFz9RH4 from 23.02.2026) · (source: Lead Gen Jay, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJbLvafsCx4 from 14.06.2024)
- **Fresh data hunger:** stale triggers mean false “why now” stories—there is no set-and-forget list; someone must refresh signals or recycle with new angles on a sane cadence—not weekly harassment. (source: Chris Ritson, research/other/chris-ritson-systems.md from 2026) · (source: Jordan Crawford, https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/inverting-your-go-to-market-strategy-thinking-with-jordan-crawford-ep-55/transcript from 17.05.2025)
- **Local nuance risk:** Suwandi Soh gives a strong baseline for Indonesia (trust, compliance, channels). Tactics tuned for Western, high-volume, low-touch outbound from other experts can **collide** with relationship-first, high-trust SEA buying reality. Treat Western defaults as **unproven** in-region until local feedback says otherwise—monitor replies, referrals, and WhatsApp tone weekly. (source: Suwandi Soh, research/other/suwandi-soh-indo-gtm.md from 2025)
- **We don't yet have statistically clean reply-rate benchmarks per HR sub-vertical** in these dossiers—leadership still has to bake testing into the forecast, not outsource it to vibes.
